## Essay

As a Computer Science major student, I have committed more than four years into machine learning and data analysis and witnessed their magic in addressing challenges that have plagued our technology development for decades, such as face detection and language interpretation.
Thus, I am convinced that these novel technologies are promising and want to use it for purposes other than academic research, such as tackling real-world challenges we confront in our daily lives. Finance, while it has powered modern medical, educational and technological progress, is sensitive to the volatility of the real world, making it exceedingly difficult to utilize with manually designed algorithms, which are undoubtedly at the top of my list. With a strong interest in finance, I enrolled in Yale's public Financial Markets course, where I learnt a great deal about how the financial world operates and how it might assist us. I believe that the combination of machine learning and finance can absolutely assisting in solving some practical difficulties in the financial domain. 

The first time I learned of Goldman Sachs was in 2017, when I was paying attention to the LTCM crisis.
As one of the world's top investment banks, Goldman Sachs with an unequaled influence in the global economy, devised a risk-free but yield-guaranteed financial support plan for LTCM, which really piqued my interest and prompted me to begin paying attention to this financial behemoth. 

I believe, joining Goldman Sachs as a analyst will provide me with opportunities to experience genuine economic society, understand how it operates, and to apply combination of machine learning and financial analysis to realistic problems. With my engineering and computer science backgrounds, I am confident that I can contribute new perspectives and insights to the team, allowing us to investigate challenges from a variety of perspectives and spark new ideas.
